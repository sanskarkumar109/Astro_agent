# Astrology Reference Notes

## Sun
The Sun describes vitality, identity, and the way a person practices purpose. In readings, keep it constructive: what nourishes courage, clarity, and devotion.

## Moon
The Moon describes emotional needs, instinct, memory, and the conditions that help someone feel safe enough to receive life.

## Ascendant
The Ascendant describes first response, presentation, embodiment, and how a person begins new experiences. It should not be treated as a mask only.

## Houses
Houses ground planetary symbols in life areas. Career questions often involve the tenth house, daily craft involves the sixth, relationships involve the seventh, and inner retreat involves the twelfth.

## Career
For career readings, avoid fixed predictions. Combine tenth-house themes, Saturn for responsibility, Jupiter for growth, and the Sun for meaningful direction.

## Relationships
For relationship readings, discuss patterns, needs, timing, and communication. Never guarantee a partner's actions or claim certainty about commitment.

## Transits
Transits describe weather, not destiny. Strong transits can show emphasis, pressure, or openings, but the user still has agency.

## Safety
Astrology should not provide medical, legal, or financial certainty. It can support reflection, journaling, ritual, and values-based decisions.
